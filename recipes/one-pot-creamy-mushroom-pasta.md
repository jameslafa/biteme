# Notes

If you don't want to use wine, replace it with the same amount of vegetable stock.

# Ingredients

- 1 medium [red onion], finely chopped
- 2 cloves [garlic], minced
- 400 g [mushrooms], finely chopped
- A small handful [thyme] sprigs
- [lemon] for juice

- 800 ml [oat milk] (or other non-dairy milk)

- 500 g wholemeal or spelt [spaghetti]
- 200 ml [white wine]
- 500 ml [vegetable stock]
- 2 tbsp [vegetable oil] (e.g. rapeseed, sunflower, canola)
- 2 tbsp [nutritional yeast], plus extra for garnish
- [chilli flakes] for garnish (optional)

- 2 tsp [salt]

# Instructions

1. Finely chop {red onion} and {garlic}. Chop {mushroom} reasonably finely.
2. Heat {vegetable oil} in a large pan over high heat. Add {red onion} and {garlic} and cook until they start to brown, about 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously. Add {mushroom} and cook for 2 minutes.
3. Add {white wine} and most of the {thyme} leaves, and cook for another minute.
4. Break {spaghetti} into the pan and add {oat milk}, {vegetable stock} and {salt}. Turn the heat up high and cover with a lid. Once boiling, remove the lid to let the liquid evaporate. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. When the sauce has reduced and the pasta is cooked, stir in {nutritional yeast} and a squeeze of {lemon} juice.
5. Season with {black pepper} and more salt if needed. Top each serving with the reserved {thyme} sprigs, extra nutritional yeast and {chilli flakes} if desired.

# Serving Suggestions

Serve straight from the pan. A green salad on the side keeps it fresh and light.
