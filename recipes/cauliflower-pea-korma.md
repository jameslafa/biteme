# Notes

You can substitute broccoli for the cauliflower if you prefer. Remove the chilli seeds for a milder dish.

# Ingredients

## Fresh

- 1 head cauliflower (about 600 g), cut into small florets
- 1 large red pepper, deseeded and finely chopped
- 4 spring onions, finely sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 thumb fresh ginger, finely chopped
- 1/2 fresh red chilli, finely sliced
- Fresh coriander for garnish

## Pantry

- 250 g frozen peas
- 1 tin (400 ml) coconut milk
- 1 tin (400 g) chopped tomatoes
- 1 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil (e.g. rapeseed, sunflower, canola)
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- Juice of 1/2 lime
- Flaked almonds for garnish

## Spices

- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 1/2 tbsp curry powder
- 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 1 1/2 tsp sea salt
- Chilli flakes for garnish

# Instructions

1. Place {frozen peas} in a bowl of boiling water to thaw.
2. Heat {oil} in a large pan over high heat. Once hot, add {garlic}, {ginger}, {red chilli} and {spring onions}. Cook for 1 1/2 minutes, stirring regularly.
3. Add {cauliflower} florets, {red pepper} and {cumin seeds}. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring regularly.
4. Drain the peas. Add the peas along with {coconut milk}, {chopped tomatoes}, {curry powder}, {black pepper}, {salt} and {maple syrup}. Bring to the boil, then reduce to a simmer for 2 minutes.
5. Remove from the heat and stir in the {lime} juice.
6. Serve topped with {coriander}, {flaked almonds} and {chilli flakes}.

# Serving Suggestions

Serve with basmati or brown rice and warm naan bread.
