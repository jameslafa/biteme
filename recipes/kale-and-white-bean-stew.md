# Notes

This stew actually tastes even better the next day as the flavours develop. Keeps in the fridge for 3 to 4 days and freezes well in portions for easy future meals.

# Ingredients

## Fresh

- 1 onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 carrot, diced
- 1 stick celery, diced
- 120 g kale, stems removed and leaves roughly chopped
- Juice of 1/2 lemon

## Pantry

- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tin (400 g) chopped tomatoes
- 1 tin (400 g) white beans (cannellini or butter beans), drained and rinsed
- 500 ml vegetable broth

## Spices

- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1/2 tsp chilli flakes (optional, adjust to taste)
- Salt and pepper to taste

# Instructions

1. Heat the {olive oil} in a large pot over medium heat. Add the {onion}, {carrot}, and {celery}, and cook for about 5 to 7 minutes until softened.
2. Add the {garlic}, {smoked paprika}, {cumin}, and {chilli flakes}. Stir for about a minute until fragrant.
3. Pour in the {chopped tomatoes} and {vegetable broth}. Give it a good stir and bring to a simmer.
4. Add the {white beans} and let it simmer for about 10 minutes so the flavours come together.
5. Toss in the {kale} and stir it through. Let it cook for another 5 to 7 minutes until the kale is tender but still has a bit of bite.
6. Season with {salt}, {pepper}, and the {lemon} juice. Taste and adjust as needed.

# Serving Suggestions

Serve with a chunk of crusty bread for dipping. Drizzle with good olive oil and a sprinkle of nutritional yeast for a savoury, cheesy flavour.
