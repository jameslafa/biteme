# Notes

This comes together in 20 minutes — prep the vegetables while the water boils. If the noodles start sticking after draining, rinse them with cold water. Add teriyaki sauce gradually to taste — different brands vary in sweetness and saltiness.

# Ingredients

## Fresh

- 3 bunches bok choy, chopped, whites and greens separated
- 4 cloves garlic, diced
- 1 bunch spring onions, chopped, whites and greens separated
- 225 g mushrooms, chopped small

## Pantry

- 2 bundles soba noodles
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 80 ml teriyaki sauce, or to taste

## Spices

- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- Red pepper flakes to taste
- Sesame seeds for serving

# Instructions

1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. While waiting, prep the vegetables. Add {soba noodles} and cook for 3–5 minutes until tender. Drain and rinse with cold water.
2. Meanwhile, heat {sesame oil} in a skillet over medium heat. Add {garlic}, {mushrooms}, whites of the {bok choy}, whites of the {spring onions}, {white pepper} and {red pepper flakes}. Cook for about 5 minutes until the mushrooms have released their liquid and it has cooked off.
3. Add 2 tablespoons of {teriyaki sauce} and cook for 1 more minute.
4. Add the soba noodles, greens of the {bok choy} and the rest of the {teriyaki sauce}. Toss until everything is coated and heated through.
5. Serve topped with remaining {spring onions} and {sesame seeds}.
